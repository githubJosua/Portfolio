// WHY SHOULD WE CARE

Manifesto

Many aspects of our lives are already heavily shaped by algorithms; facilitating communication, curating our intake of information, and guiding us to our next destinations. Still today, most of this is happening through the glowing screens that accompany us wherever we go. But right now, we are experiencing a curious moment, where those exact algorithms are increasingly diffusing into the physical environment around us. They sense and analyze but also act and influence our environment.

At the frontline of this transition are the private companies that already control the digital space. They look at the built environment as another platform to be automized, optimized, and monetized. "Data is the new currency" and "real time is already too late" are their slogans. And at the heart of it are algorithms designed to predict and influence human behavior. They predict the future at the cost of tomorrow.

So, why should we care? We should care because it affects all of us. The phenomenon is everywhere, it's always, and it's involuntary. It is foolish to ask if it will happen, the transition is inevitable. But we can still ask how it will happen, and by asking find new alternatives. This proposal is one such alternative. It centers around three keywords: Agency, Transparency, and Friction. The agency of the human, the transparency of the system, and the friction between them, are the core principles to guide the project.

// HOW WILL IT WORK TOMORROW

Physical Negotiation

Urban Creatures are architectural structures, that implant themselves in the urban environment. They sense, reason, act, communicate, and negotiate. Through the cycles of these five states, they live in both; the physical and the digital space - the city of tomorrow and the internet of today. The architecture and the algorithm share the same logic.

Through these cycles, they come alive: never fixed, never static, never finished, unexpected, weird, and suspicious. They tease curiosity, ask for doubt, and invite negotiation. Human negotiation completes the creatures, creating a new type of cyborgian reality: instead of augmenting the human with technology, it augments the technology with the human.

Urban creatures sense their environment through advanced sensing technology and are therefore aware of their surrounding. They use the inventions of the global institutional surveillance apparatus to see, to hear, to smell, to touch, and to taste. They perceive their surrounding and translate their perception into computational language for analysis. This is not surveillance. It is attention. The creature listens before it acts.
Urban creatures think. Each creature is gifted a silicon brain with sufficient processing and memory capacities. It situates its sensory data with its own identity — its DNA, its objective, its location. It tries to make sense of all the impressions and reasons about their meaning. It finds patterns, builds understanding, predicts probabilities, and plans what to do next.
Urban creatures act. They influence the city around them through physical actuators. They control the lights, the motors, the valves, and the magnets and through those elements the environment. They are in the position to nudge what our senses perceive, how we move through space, and where we have access to. They create a decentralized alternative to the current development of hyper-centralized automated systems of control.
Urban creatures communicate with each other and with their environment. They share their data, their analysis, their reasoning, and their plans. They are very open and honest, with no ability to hide or lie. They communicate with each other in their own language through their connection to the local optic fibre cable network. They communicate with us humans in any of our languages through microphones and speakers. The conversation is fragmented, scattered, contradictory, and very contemporary.
The creature is not closed. Humans can enter freely at any time. The human that enters gets to experience the transparency of the system and the ability to understand the processes behind. The human can start interacting with the creature, entering into a conversation to negotiate the city. This is the cyborgian exchange, that lifts the human back into a position of power. It is lastly the augmentation of the machine with the unexpected nature of the human that defeats the logic of automation and prediction.
// HOW DOES IT WORK TODAY

Digital Negotiation

The same five steps that create the cycles of life for the creature in the city, define the cycles of evolution for the creature in the cloud. Before it's physical creation, the creature grows in a daily rhythm, guided by human voices. Everyday, for every creature, a new image is created. These images are shared on multiple platforms to receive human feedback. All the comments are read, saved, and used to make intentions for the new images. Everyone can participate by simply commenting below the images on any of the platforms below.

Every day, the system scans the internet. Comments from Bluesky, Instagram, LinkedIn and Telegram are collected and saved. The most recent voices carry the most influence, older ones fade slowly. Every comment, however small, enters the record. The collection of opinions is as absurd as it is generative.
A python code collects the creature's DNA, its entire feedback history, and a curated catalog of architectural references and feeds it to an AI model. Based on this the AI creates the intention of the day. It selects a viewing angle and a style reference that support this intention. It then writes a precise prompt for the image generation. The prompt along with three images are given to another AI model as inputs for the image generation.
Based on the inputs from the thinking step - a prompt and three images - the AI model renders the new image of the day. The image always reminds of the inputs, the prompt, the viewing angle, the style reference, and the material reference, but it also always carries something new. It is familiar and unfamiliar at the same time - somehow uncanny, in the way Sigmund Freud describes it. Every image continues the story of the creature one day at the time.
The approved iteration is shared on different public platforms, as well as this website. The image is posted along with a short description or question to evoke new feedback. It wanders through the digital space, waiting for new voices to respond.
Once the images circulate, the negotiation begins. Everyone can enter the conversation, everyone's opinion has the same weight. Comments can be anonymous or not, they can be clear or blurry, directive or absurd. There are no rules to the game, except for: speak up if you have something to say, and don't be shy.
