As climate change is advancing, our glaciers are melting away rapidly. We are losing more and more of what is currently our biggest water storage in Switzerland. They have been the main power that formed our mountainous landscape and are still in constant motion today. How can we preserve the vanishing but rich geometries from glacial surfaces in something more permanent and less sensible?

Since one of the most defining processes of glacial surface creation is erosion through meltwater, this project lets big ice blocks be eroded and formed by water. The melting ice is used as formwork for a special concrete mixture, which hardens at subzero temperatures. While the melting of the ice advances further in spring, the final form gets uncovered.
